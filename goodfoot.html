SCARF HISTORY
The History Of Silk Scarves

Textile Experts claim that the scarf was made of cotton rather than wool and initially, it
indicated the rank of the warriors in the Chinese Emperor Cheng (Shih Huang Ti)..

The styled scarf dates back at least as far as ancient Rome where it was called sudarium
(Latin term for “sweat cloth”) the sudarium soon became a fashion accessory for both
men and women, whether it was knotted to a belt or worn round the neck.

The elegant “Croatian style” enamored the French who were thrilled with the fashion
innovation and started wearing scarves too. The French called the scarves cravatas from
the Croatian word kravata. A scarf was also used to show one’s political inclination by its
color and as such, became extremely popular.

In the 18th century, men and women alike wore neckerchiefs, no matter of the social class
they belonged to and the trend rapidly grew.. In the 19th century, the cravat became again
a fashion accessory for men and a basic item in every Man’s wardrobe. Around 1840,
single or double-knotted fringes were extremely stylish and scarves were worn both as
neck shawls and headwear for Women..

The 20th century saw different types of scarves inspired by the old trends and combined
with the new ones. Women began wearing more ornate scarves made of hand woven silks..

Silk Creates luster and beauty as its smooth fiber enables it to reflect light and give it a
silky feel and improving one’s look as it drapes beautifully and retains its shape.. The
exquisite nature of the silk material makes them provide endless fashionable possibilities
for enhancing a wardrobe.. As a head covering, a belt, sashes, an accent piece or just like
a shawl.. Silk was, for centuries, given the reputation of a luxurious and sensuous fabric,
associated as such with style, wealth and success.. “A status symbol still to this day"..

The Executive Women today wears Silk Accessories with Their Suiting’s and Separates
in place of a Man’s style ties and pocket squares..

The WW/B Woman has countless styles to select from to enhance Her Professional
Wardrobe, while contributing to Her more fashioned casual business attire when
traveling..
